## What First Principles Actually Is

First principles thinking strips a problem down to its irreducible truths — things you *know* are true — and rebuilds from there, discarding inherited assumptions  [modelthinkers](https://modelthinkers.com/mental-model/first-principle-thinking). Aristotle formalized it, Feynman lived it, Musk used it to ask "why does a rocket battery cost $80k when the raw materials cost $2k?"  [growthemind](https://growthemind.ai/blogs/better-thinking/how-to-use-first-principles-thinking-daily). The key insight: **most of what we accept as constraints are just unchallenged conventions**, not laws of physics.  [extern](https://www.extern.com/post/mental-models-for-smarter-thinking-the-first-principles-approach)

## The Syntax (Quick Reference)

Think of it as a 4-step loop you can run on *anything*:

1. **State the problem** — "I want X but I can't because Y"
2. **Deconstruct with 5 Whys** — keep asking "why is Y true?" until you hit bedrock (a fact, not an assumption)  [extern](https://www.extern.com/post/mental-models-for-smarter-thinking-the-first-principles-approach)
3. **Challenge each assumption** — ask "is this actually true, or just conventional?"  [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vmjuPrvHZdw)
4. **Rebuild from bedrock** — construct a solution using only the truths you've confirmed, not inherited patterns  [growthemind](https://growthemind.ai/blogs/better-thinking/how-to-use-first-principles-thinking-daily)

A fast mental shorthand: **Problem → Strip → Bedrock → Rebuild**

## Applying It to Mundane Daily Tasks

The power is in realizing that boring daily friction is just *un-examined assumptions*  [fs](https://fs.blog/mental-models/). Some examples:

- **"I don't have time to exercise"** → Why? → Schedule is full → Why? → Obligations fill available slots → Bedrock truth: exercise requires *movement*, not a gym slot → Rebuild: walking phone calls, standing during work  [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vmjuPrvHZdw)
- **"Cooking takes too long"** → Why? → Prep and cleanup are slow → Bedrock: nutrition is the goal, not cooking *as ritual* → Rebuild: one-pot meals, batch prep once a week
- **"I'm always distracted"** → Why? → Notifications arrive unpredictably → Bedrock: attention follows unpredictable reward (dopamine loops) → Rebuild: remove notification sources entirely rather than relying on willpower  [scratchie](https://www.scratchie.com/post/the-science-of-instant-gratification-why-immediate-feedback-works)
- **"I can't save money"** → Why? → Spending happens reactively → Bedrock: money saved is money not seen → Rebuild: automate transfer on payday before discretionary access  [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vmjuPrvHZdw)

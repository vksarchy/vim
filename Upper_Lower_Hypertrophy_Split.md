# Upper/Lower Hypertrophy Split (4 Days/Week)
**Goal:** Muscle growth | **Structure:** Upper × 2, Lower × 2 (stacked) | **Duration:** 45-60 min per session

---

## Weekly Schedule

```
Monday    → UPPER A
Tuesday   → LOWER A
Wednesday → REST / Light cardio
Thursday  → UPPER B
Friday    → LOWER B
Saturday  → Optional (active recovery, cardio, mobility)
Sunday    → REST
```

---

## Key Principles for Hypertrophy

✅ **Volume:** 12-20 sets per muscle group per week  
✅ **Rep range:** 6-12 reps (sweet spot for muscle growth)  
✅ **Rest periods:** 60-90s between sets (pump and fatigue)  
✅ **Progression:** Add weight, reps, or sets weekly  
✅ **Tempo:** Control the eccentric (lowering phase = 2-3 seconds)  

---

# UPPER A (Monday)
**Focus:** Strength & hypertrophy emphasis  
**Duration:** 45-50 minutes  

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| **Barbell Bench Press** | 4 | 6-8 | 90s | Main lift — heavy, controlled |
| **Bent-Over Barbell Rows** | 4 | 6-8 | 90s | Match bench volume, explosive pull |
| **Incline Dumbbell Press** | 3 | 8-10 | 75s | Upper chest, shoulder development |
| **Dumbbell Rows (single arm)** | 3 | 8-10 | 75s | Unilateral balance, lat activation |
| **Face Pulls** | 3 | 12-15 | 60s | Rear delts, shoulder health |
| **Barbell Curls** | 3 | 8-10 | 60s | Arm hypertrophy, cumulative volume |
| **Tricep Rope Pushdowns** | 2 | 10-12 | 60s | Finish with pump work |

---

### UPPER A Exercise Details

#### 1. Barbell Bench Press
**Target:** Chest, front shoulders, triceps  
**Form:**
- Feet flat on floor, shoulder blades retracted
- Grip slightly wider than shoulder width
- Lower bar to mid-chest (pause 1 sec)
- Press explosively, lock out elbows
- 2-3 second eccentric (lowering)

**Progression:** Add 2.5-5 lbs weekly if hitting 6+ reps consistently

---

#### 2. Bent-Over Barbell Rows
**Target:** Back thickness, lats, biceps  
**Form:**
- Hinge at hips (~45° angle), slight knee bend
- Chest near parallel to floor
- Bar starts at shin level
- Row to chest, elbows drive back
- Squeeze shoulder blades, control descent
- Keep core tight (no lower back rounding)

**Progression:** Match bench press progression (+2.5-5 lbs weekly)

---

#### 3. Incline Dumbbell Press
**Target:** Upper chest, front delts, triceps  
**Form:**
- Set bench to 30-45° incline
- Dumbbells at shoulder height
- Press up and slightly forward
- Elbows 45° from body
- 2-second pause at top

**Progression:** Increase weight or add reps each week

---

#### 4. Dumbbell Single-Arm Rows
**Target:** Back, unilateral strength, core stability  
**Form:**
- One knee on bench, opposite foot on floor
- Row dumbbell to hip, elbow past ribs
- Squeeze lat hard, prevent rotation
- Alternate sides, full reps each

**Progression:** Heavier dumbbells, control tempo

---

#### 5. Face Pulls
**Target:** Rear delts, upper back, shoulder health  
**Form:**
- Rope attachment on cable machine
- Stand facing machine, rope at eye level
- Pull rope toward face, elbows high
- Externally rotate shoulders at top
- Squeeze rear delts, slow eccentric

**Progression:** Increase weight, add reps (lower fatigue, high frequency)

---

#### 6. Barbell Curls
**Target:** Bicep peak & mass  
**Form:**
- Feet hip-width, slight knee bend
- Bar at hip level, elbows locked at sides
- Curl bar to shoulder level
- Squeeze 1-2 seconds at top
- Lower with control (3 seconds), minimal swing

**Progression:** Add weight or reps weekly

---

#### 7. Tricep Rope Pushdowns
**Target:** Tricep finish, pump  
**Form:**
- Cable machine, rope attachment
- Elbows locked at sides
- Push rope down, extend fully
- Squeeze triceps, high reps (10-12)

**Progression:** Increase weight or volume

---

# LOWER A (Tuesday)
**Focus:** Squat emphasis, posterior chain  
**Duration:** 45-50 minutes  

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| **Barbell Back Squat** | 4 | 6-8 | 2-3 min | Main lift — heavy, full recovery |
| **Romanian Deadlift (RDL)** | 4 | 8-10 | 90s | Hamstring & lower back focus |
| **Leg Press** | 3 | 8-10 | 90s | Quad volume, joint-friendly |
| **Walking Lunges (Dumbbell)** | 3 | 10-12 each | 75s | Single-leg strength, glute drive |
| **Leg Curls (machine)** | 3 | 10-12 | 60s | Hamstring isolation, pump |
| **Calf Raises (barbell or machine)** | 3 | 12-15 | 60s | Often neglected, full ROM |

---

### LOWER A Exercise Details

#### 1. Barbell Back Squat
**Target:** Quads, glutes, entire lower body  
**Form:**
- Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
- Bar on upper back (not neck), chest up
- Descend hips back, knees track over toes
- Go to parallel or deeper (depth = growth)
- Drive through heels, stand powerfully
- 2-3 second eccentric (control the descent)

**Progression:** Add 5-10 lbs weekly if hitting depth

---

#### 2. Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
**Target:** Hamstrings, glutes, lower back  
**Form:**
- Feet hip-width, bar at hip level
- Keep knees nearly straight (slight bend)
- Hinge at hips, push butt back
- Feel hamstring stretch, bar travels close to legs
- Drive hips forward to return
- Never round lower back

**Progression:** Add 5 lbs weekly, control tempo

---

#### 3. Leg Press
**Target:** Quads, glutes, overall leg mass  
**Form:**
- Feet shoulder-width on platform
- Lower until knees ~90° (don't go too deep)
- Press through heels, extend fully (don't lock)
- Smooth, controlled motion
- Full ROM without rounding lower back

**Progression:** Add weight or reps each session

---

#### 4. Walking Lunges (Dumbbell)
**Target:** Quads, glutes, single-leg strength, balance  
**Form:**
- Dumbbells at sides
- Step forward, front knee bends 90°
- Back knee drops toward ground
- Push through front heel to stand and step
- Alternate legs, controlled pace

**Progression:** Heavier dumbbells or longer distance

---

#### 5. Leg Curls (Machine)
**Target:** Hamstring isolation, peak contraction  
**Form:**
- Lie face down, legs extended
- Curl heels toward glutes
- Squeeze hamstrings hard at top (2 sec)
- Lower with control, never lock out fully
- Full ROM, consistent tempo

**Progression:** Increase weight or add reps

---

#### 6. Calf Raises
**Target:** Soleus & gastrocnemius (calf muscles)  
**Form:**
- Barbell on upper back OR standing machine
- Rise up onto toes, full extension
- Pause 1-2 seconds at top
- Lower slowly, stretch calf at bottom
- Full ROM every rep

**Progression:** Add weight, increase reps

---

# UPPER B (Thursday)
**Focus:** Hypertrophy emphasis, higher reps  
**Duration:** 45-50 minutes  

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| **Dumbbell Bench Press** | 4 | 8-10 | 75s | Variation, chest hypertrophy |
| **Dumbbell Pullovers** | 3 | 8-10 | 75s | Chest/lat cross-over, unique stimulus |
| **Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldowns** | 4 | 8-12 | 75s | Back width, lat development |
| **Chest-Supported Rows** | 3 | 10-12 | 75s | Back thickness, chest support |
| **Lateral Raise (Dumbbell)** | 3 | 12-15 | 60s | Shoulder width/caps |
| **Dumbbell Flyes** | 3 | 10-12 | 60s | Chest pump, stretch emphasis |
| **EZ-Bar Skull Crushers** | 3 | 8-10 | 60s | Tricep mass builder |

---

### UPPER B Exercise Details

#### 1. Dumbbell Bench Press
**Target:** Chest, front delts, triceps (variation)  
**Form:**
- Incline or flat bench
- Dumbbells at shoulder level
- Press up, slight inward angle at top
- Dumbbells close together (pecs engaged)
- Full ROM, pause 1 sec at bottom

**Progression:** Heavier dumbbells week-to-week

---

#### 2. Dumbbell Pullovers
**Target:** Chest/lat cross-over, rib cage expansion  
**Form:**
- Lie perpendicular on flat bench (chest/shoulders on bench)
- Single dumbbell with both hands, arms slightly bent
- Lower weight behind head in arc motion
- Feel chest and lat stretch
- Pull weight back over chest, controlled

**Progression:** Increase weight or ROM

---

#### 3. Pull-Ups / Lat Pulldowns
**Target:** Back width, lat development, biceps  

**Pull-Ups Form:**
- Grip shoulder-width or slightly wider
- Dead hang, full extension at bottom
- Pull chest toward bar, elbows back
- Pause 1 sec, lower with control
- Strict form (no kipping)

**Lat Pulldown Form (if pull-ups too hard):**
- Grip shoulder-width, knees under thigh pad
- Pull bar to upper chest
- Elbows drive down and back
- Squeeze lats, controlled ascent

**Progression:** Add reps, or add weight to belt for pull-ups

---

#### 4. Chest-Supported Rows
**Target:** Back thickness, isolation, reduced lower back stress  
**Form:**
- Machine or incline bench with barbell
- Chest supported at angle
- Row bar to chest, elbows back
- Squeeze shoulder blades hard
- Lower with control

**Progression:** Add weight or reps

---

#### 5. Lateral Raises
**Target:** Shoulder medial delts, width  
**Form:**
- Dumbbells at sides, slight bend in elbows
- Raise arms to shoulder height (T position)
- Pause 1 sec at top
- Lower with control (eccentric emphasis)
- Minimal swing, strict form

**Progression:** Increase weight, add reps

---

#### 6. Dumbbell Flyes
**Target:** Chest stretch, pec activation  
**Form:**
- Flat bench, dumbbells at chest level
- Arms slightly bent, elbows locked
- Arc arms out and down (fly motion)
- Feel chest stretch at bottom
- Squeeze pecs, pull dumbbells back together
- Slow eccentric (3 seconds)

**Progression:** Heavier weight, longer ROM

---

#### 7. EZ-Bar Skull Crushers
**Target:** Tricep mass, lockout strength  
**Form:**
- Lying on flat bench
- EZ-bar at arm's length above chest
- Lower bar toward forehead/behind head (bend elbows)
- Elbows stay relatively fixed
- Extend bar back up, squeeze triceps
- Controlled tempo throughout

**Progression:** Increase weight or reps weekly

# LOWER B (Friday)
**Focus:** Deadlift emphasis, glute/hamstring priority  
**Duration:** 45-50 minutes  

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| **Conventional Deadlift** | 4 | 5-6 | 2-3 min | Main lift — heavy, full recovery |
| **Bulgarian Split Squats** | 3 | 8-10 each | 90s | Single-leg quad hypertrophy |
| **Leg Press** | 3 | 10-12 | 90s | Additional quad volume |
| **Hip Thrusts (Barbell)** | 3 | 10-12 | 75s | Glute peak, hypertrophy |
| **Leg Extensions (machine)** | 3 | 10-12 | 60s | Quad isolation, pump |
| **Seated Calf Raises** | 3 | 12-15 | 60s | Soleus focus, different angle |

---

### LOWER B Exercise Details

#### 1. Conventional Deadlift
**Target:** Entire posterior chain, glutes, hamstrings, back  
**Form:**
- Feet hip-width, bar over midfoot
- Shoulders over bar, chest up
- Pull bar close to legs (shin to thigh)
- Drive through heels, hips and shoulders rise together
- Stand tall, full lockout at top
- Controlled descent (2-3 sec)
- Reset between reps (touch and go optional)

**Progression:** Add 5-10 lbs weekly

---

#### 2. Bulgarian Split Squats
**Target:** Single-leg quad strength, glute hypertrophy  
**Form:**
- Back foot elevated on bench behind you
- Front foot forward (lunge position)
- Lower front knee toward ground (90° bend)
- Drive through front heel to return
- Dumbbells at sides or goblet style
- Full ROM, explosive up phase

**Progression:** Heavier dumbbells, increase reps

---

#### 3. Leg Press (Higher Reps)
**Target:** Quad volume, glute contribution  
**Form:**
- Feet shoulder-width
- Lower until knees ~90°
- Press through full foot
- Higher reps than LOWER A (10-12)
- Less rest = metabolic stress for growth

**Progression:** Increase weight or reps

---

#### 4. Hip Thrusts (Barbell)
**Target:** Glute peak, hypertrophy, posterior chain  
**Form:**
- Upper back on bench, feet flat on floor
- Barbell across hips
- Drive through heels, thrust hips up
- Squeeze glutes hard at top (1-2 sec)
- Lower with control
- Full ROM, high reps = pump and growth

**Progression:** Add weight or reps weekly

---

#### 5. Leg Extensions (Machine)
**Target:** Quad isolation, pump, finisher  
**Form:**
- Seated, legs under pad
- Extend legs fully, pause 1 sec
- Control descent, never fully lock out
- High reps for metabolic stress
- Full ROM each rep

**Progression:** Increase weight or reps

---

#### 6. Seated Calf Raises
**Target:** Soleus (lower calf), different angle from standing  
**Form:**
- Seated, weight on knees
- Raise heels, full calf contraction
- Pause 1-2 sec at top
- Lower with stretch, full ROM
- Higher reps for pump

**Progression:** Add weight or reps

# Progression Strategy (Weekly)

**Week 1:** Establish baseline weights, focus on form  
**Week 2+:** Follow these priority rules:

1. **If you hit top range of reps (6-8 / 8-10 / 10-12):** Add weight next session (+2.5-5 lbs)
2. **If you miss reps:** Repeat weight, aim for all reps next time
3. **If you hit middle reps:** Add 1-2 reps the next session, then increase weight
4. **Deload:** Every 4-6 weeks, reduce weight by ~20%, focus on form and recovery

**Example progression:**
- Week 1: Bench 185 × 6 reps
- Week 2: Bench 185 × 7 reps
- Week 3: Bench 185 × 8 reps (hit top range)
- Week 4: Bench 190 × 6 reps (new weight, reset reps)

# FAQ

**Q: Can I do Upper A then Upper B back-to-back?**  
A: Not recommended. Stacking allows CNS recovery and better quality. Stick to the schedule.

**Q: Should I eat before the gym?**  
A: Yes. Carbs + protein 1-2 hours before. Example: banana + Greek yogurt, or oatmeal + whey.

**Q: What if I miss a day?**  
A: Don't skip within the same session pair (don't do Upper A and B without rest). Adjust your week.

**Q: How long until I see results?**  
A: 4 weeks noticeable strength gains, 8 weeks visible muscle growth. Stay consistent.

**Q: Can I add cardio?**  
A: Light cardio (20-30 min) on rest days is fine. Don't run marathons (impacts recovery).

**Q: I'm sore — should I train?**  
A: DOMS is normal. Train through it (it goes away after set 1). If it's sharp pain, skip.

# Final Notes

This split is **designed for hypertrophy on a comeback trajectory**. You'll hit each muscle group 2x per week with high volume, adequate rest, and progressive overload. The stacked structure (Upper/Lower days back-to-back) matches a typical 5-day work week and gives you 2 rest days for recovery.

**Consistency > perfection.** Stick to this for 8-12 weeks before switching programs.
